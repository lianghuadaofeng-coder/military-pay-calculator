Warrant Officer Pay 2026: W-1 to W-5 Charts

Updated 2026-06-08

Warrant officers are the military's technical specialists, and their pay reflects it: in 2026 a W-1 starts at $4,057/month, while a senior W-5 earns up to $13,308/month — plus the same tax-free BAH and BAS as other officers.

W-5 pay begins at 20 years of service — chief warrant officers five are, by definition, deep-career specialists. Note that W-2 at 10 years ($6,282) out-earns an O-1 with the same time.

How warrant pay compares

  • vs senior enlisted: a new W-1 ($4,057) starts above an E-6 with 8 years ($4,612).
  • vs commissioned officers: mid-career warrants (W-3/W-4) overlap with O-3/O-4 pay, and prior-enlisted warrants typically carry many years of service into the table — meaning they enter at a high column, not the left edge.
  • Allowances: warrant officers receive the officer BAS rate ($328.48 in 2026) and BAH at their own W-grade rates.

Frequently asked questions

How much does a warrant officer make in 2026?

From $4,057/month for a new W-1 to $13,308/month for a W-5 with 38 years, plus tax-free BAH and BAS.

Do warrant officers get officer BAS?

Yes — warrant officers receive the officer BAS rate ($328.48/month in 2026) and BAH at their own W-grade rates.

Do warrant officers out-earn enlisted?

Generally yes at the same point in a career — a starting W-1 ($4,057) earns more than an E-6 over 8 years ($4,612).
